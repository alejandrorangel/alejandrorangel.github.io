## Hello!

I'm Alejandro Rangel, a **Technical Engineering Lead** at **Fender Musical Instruments Corporation** based in Los Angeles, California. I've been coding since I was 10 years old, and I've spent over 15 years building software across mobile, web, cloud, and AI — from research labs to products used by millions of musicians worldwide.

## What I Do

I lead technical engineering at Fender, where I've been for over 10 years. My journey here has taken me through four roles — Senior Mobile/Web Developer, Platform Engineer, Senior Software Engineer, and now Technical Engineering Lead — giving me a deep understanding of every layer of our stack: iOS (Swift, Objective-C), Android (Java), Node.js, AWS, and web technologies.

Along the way I've shipped products used by millions of musicians, including [**Fender Play**](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.fender.play) (the guitar learning platform), [**Fender Tune**](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.fender.tuner), [**Fender Tone**](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.fender.tone), and the **Tone Master Pro** digital amp modeler.

Lately I've been focused on AI-assisted development, building internal tooling and frameworks that harness LLMs to improve engineering velocity and code quality across teams. This builds on a long-standing interest in AI/ML — I completed Stanford's Machine Learning certification in 2023 and have been deep in the generative AI space since.

## Research & Publications

Before industry, I spent time in academia and research. I was an Undergraduate Research Assistant at **UC Irvine's Laboratory for Ubiquitous Computing and Interaction**, where I built Android applications involving augmented reality, geolocation, and activity estimation. I also developed research applications at **CICESE Research Center** in Mexico.

That work led to two published papers:

- [*MOSOCO: A Mobile Assistive Tool to Support Children with Autism Practicing Social Skills in Real-Life Situations*](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2212776.2223802) — published at **CHI'12**, one of the top conferences in human-computer interaction. MOSOCO used augmented reality to help children with autism practice social skills in real-life situations, and our study showed it increased both the quantity and quality of social interactions.
- [*Self-Configurable Activities in Activity-Aware Computing: The Case of Autism*](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-642-35377-2_28) — published at **UCAMI'11**, introducing the concept of self-configurable activities for context-aware applications.

The intersection of human-computer interaction and real-world impact is something that still drives how I think about building software. I also hold an HCI certification from the Interaction Design Foundation.

## Approach

I'm a generalist by conviction. Rather than going deep on a single language or framework, I focus on understanding the patterns and principles that translate across platforms. Mobile, backend, cloud infrastructure, AI tooling — I pick the right tool for the problem, not the other way around.

The most valuable skill I've developed isn't any specific technology. It's the ability to adapt, learn fast, and find the most effective solution regardless of the stack.

I'm also a contributor to open source — I've participated in the **Apache OpenOffice** project as a documenter and tester. I'm a native Spanish speaker and professionally fluent in English.
